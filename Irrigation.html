    • What is Natural Irrigation?

    Irrigation water can come from groundwater, through springs or wells, surface water, through rivers, lakes, or reservoirs, or even other sources, such as treated wastewater or desalinated water. Water leaking from water channels or pipes is a possibility. The water permeates the subsoil and can irrigate the neighboring crops. This water loss may also raise the level of subsurface water, which benefits crops by supplying them with nutrients. In this sense, natural sub-surface irrigation is defined as underground watering that is performed naturally. Here at Triple Y palm Oil we make use of natural irrigation.
